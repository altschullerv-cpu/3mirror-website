01 The analysis engine

3Mirror

Two channels. Never mixed. That's the point.

Every voice AI system today does the same thing: it listens to your words and your tone, blends them together, and gives you a single label — "happy," "angry," "stressed."

The problem? Blending destroys the most valuable signal. When someone says "I'm excited about this deal" but their voice tells a different story — that gap is the insight. Merge the channels and the gap disappears.

3Mirror doesn't merge. It runs two independent streams — what you say and how you say it — and measures the distance between them. Word by word. Millisecond by millisecond. The gap isn't noise. It's the product.

MIRROR 01

What is said

The semantic layer

Your words are transcribed and analyzed: what topics are you discussing, what claims are you making, how certain do your statements sound — based purely on the text. This layer extracts meaning from language.

OutputA timestamped map of what the speaker is communicating.
MIRROR 02

How it is said

The acoustic layer

The same audio is processed separately for voice characteristics: pitch, tremor, speech rate, pauses, dynamic range. These are extracted as numbers on the speaker's own device — the audio never leaves.

OutputA timestamped map of how the speaker sounds, word by word.
MIRROR 03

Who is speaking

The context layer

Who is this person? A first-time caller or a returning client? In a job interview or a therapy session? Context determines what a divergence means. The same vocal tremor means something different in a wedding toast than in a sales negotiation.

OutputAn interpretation frame that turns raw divergence into insight.

02 The measurement foundation

Reverse Generative Calibration (RGC)

The ruler that makes the measurement possible.

To measure how far a voice diverges from its words, you need a reference point — a voice that has zero divergence. Where do you find one?

You build it. A text-to-speech model reading words without any emotional instructions has no feelings, no hidden agenda, no internal state. It can't say one thing and mean another. Its output is pure alignment between words and voice — a natural zero point.

RGC feeds hundreds of phrases into multiple TTS models, extracts the acoustic signature of that zero point, and builds a universal measurement scale. The scale works instantly — no training data from the speaker, no waiting period, no personal recordings needed.

Real human speech is then measured against this scale: from 0.0 (full alignment) to 1.0 (maximum divergence between words and voice).

This is not emotion recognition. Emotion AI asks: what is the speaker feeling? 3Mirror asks: does the voice match what the words are saying? Direction labels are coordinates on a calibrated scale — not emotions assigned by a model.

03 Where it applies

Applications

Wherever the gap between words and voice carries information — from clinical signal to behavioral integrity.

Deepfake Detection

A synthetic voice is acoustically perfect but behaviorally flat — it lacks the micro-variations of a real internal state. RGC detects this absence. Unlike artifact-based detectors, this approach doesn't degrade as generative models improve. It gets more relevant.

Voice AI

The risk-analytics layer that runs behind any conversational AI. Not a replacement for the dialogue — a parallel channel that flags when the speaker's tone doesn't match their words. Think of it as a background analyst, not a faster cashier.

Clinical Monitoring

Speech patterns shift years before a clinical diagnosis appears. A reproducible measurement scale makes longitudinal tracking possible — not as a diagnosis, but as an early signal that something is changing.

Psychotherapy Support

A client says "I'm fine" — but their voice says otherwise. 3Mirror quantifies that gap and shows the clinician exactly where it occurs. Not a diagnosis. Not a replacement for judgment. A calibrated signal the clinician couldn't see before.

HR & Negotiations

High-stakes conversations carry information in how things are said, not just what. Divergence analysis surfaces that information — measurably, not impressionistically.

Communication Training

Define a target vocal profile for a specific communicative goal. Measure the distance between current delivery and the target. Train toward it with real-time, quantitative feedback.
